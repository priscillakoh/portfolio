Portfolio

"yet" is a piece made through calligraphy ink, watercolor, and a collection of achromatic images to properly portray the personal emotions of a former Korean comfort woman. Comfort women were women around the age of 12-19 who were brutally forced into becoming sex slaves for the Japanese Imperial Army during WWII. A majority of the women came from Korea, one of the many places Japan had colonized. The lines on the woman’s face represent her pain as well as her healing process to feel “human” again. The hands represent empathy from other comfort women; the collage make up a silhouette of a comfort woman’s ghost symbolizing stories that were silenced, and the butterfly symbolizes hope. This piece is titled yet because they have yet to receive a sincere apology from the Japanese government, have yet to reclaim their peace, and have yet to fully recover from the atrocities they faced.
